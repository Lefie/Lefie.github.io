Iran-Iraq War
Iran Iraq War was fought between Iran and Iraq and lasted from 1980 to 1988.The war began when Iraq invaded Iran. Some causes included territorial, religious and political dispute.The conflict resulted in significant loss of life, with an estimate of up to a million people killed on both sides.
Operation Ramadan was a major offensive launched by Iran against Iraq during the Iran-Iraq War. It began on July 13, 1982, and was intended to break the stalemate that had characterized the conflict up to that point.It marked the first large-scale offensive by Iran against Iraq and demonstrated the Iranian military's growing capabilities. It also highlighted the brutality of the Iran-Iraq War, with both sides using chemical weapons and engaging in massive, destructive battles.
Operation Nasr (also known as Tariq al-Qods) was a military operation launched by Iraq during the Iran-Iraq War. It began on April 5, 1987, and was intended to recapture the city of Basra from Iranian forces, who had been occupying the city since 1982.The fighting was intense and brutal, with both sides suffering heavy losses. The Iranians eventually withdrew from the city, but at a high cost.
